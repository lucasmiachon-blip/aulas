# PART 1: BENCHMARK SYSTEM

## Scoring Philosophy

**Total Score = 6 benchmarks × 5 points = 30 max**

Each benchmark evaluates a distinct dimension:
1. **NEJM** = Scientific rigor
2. **JACC** = Clinical actionability  
3. **ESC** = Regulatory compliance
4. **Reynolds** = Signal vs noise
5. **Tufte** = Data-ink efficiency
6. **Duarte** = Narrative impact

---

### 1. vs NEJM (Scientific Rigor) — Score: X/5

**Criteria:**
- **Precision:** Números com 2+ decimais quando relevante (HR 2.47, não "~2.5")
- **Units:** SEMPRE especificar (mg/dL, mmol/L, HR vs RR)
- **Source:** Citação completa (Autor+Ano, não "estudos mostram")
- **Graphics:** Escalas claras, legendas completas, CIs quando aplicável

**Critical failures (auto -0.5 each):**
- ❌ Aproximações numéricas sem justificativa
- ❌ Unidades omitidas
- ❌ Fonte vaga ou ausente
- ❌ Gráficos sem escala/legenda

**Deductions:**
- Aproximações: -0.2 por ocorrência
- Unidades omitidas: -0.3
- Fonte não citada: -0.3
- Gráficos sem escala: -0.3

**Scoring guide:**
- 5/5: Publication-ready, NEJM aceitaria
- 4/5: Excelente, pequenos ajustes
- 3/5: Bom, falta rigor em detalhes
- 2/5: Aceitável para grand rounds
- 1/5: Informação presente mas apresentação amadora
- 0/5: Dados ausentes/incorretos

---

### 2. vs JACC Graphics (Clinical Impact) — Score: X/5

**Criteria:**
- **Simplicidade:** Mensagem compreensível em <10 segundos
- **Contraste:** Hierarquia visual imediata (3+ níveis)
- **Quantificação:** HR, RR, NNT, p-values presentes
- **Actionable:** Decisão clara (dose, droga, meta)

**Critical failures (auto -0.5 each):**
- ❌ Exemplos genéricos (não concretos)
- ❌ Benefício sem quantificação
- ❌ Teoria sem aplicação prática
- ❌ Recomendação ambígua

**Deductions:**
- Exemplo abstrato: -0.4
- Hierarquia pobre: -0.3
- Dados não interpretados: -0.3
- Recomendação vaga: -0.3

**Scoring guide:**
- 5/5: Resident pode implementar amanhã
- 4/5: Guidance clara, gaps menores
- 3/5: Informativo mas requer inferência
- 2/5: Educacional mas não acionável
- 1/5: Conhecimento teórico apenas
- 0/5: Sem aplicação prática

### 3. vs ESC Guidelines (Regulatory Standard) — Score: X/5

**Criteria:**
- **Layout profissional:** Zero amadorismo
- **Citações completas:** Class + Level of Evidence
- **Hierarquia regulatória:** Guidelines > consensus > opinião
- **Contexto geográfico:** Balance SBC + internacional

**Critical failures (auto -0.5 each):**
- ❌ Sem citação de guidelines
- ❌ Class/Level ausente em recomendações formais
- ❌ Visual amador (fonts inconsistentes, spacing)
- ❌ Guideline local ignorada

**Deductions:**
- Guidelines parafraseadas: -0.3
- Inconsistências visuais: -0.3
- Sem contexto brasileiro: -0.4
- Layout amador: -0.4

**Scoring guide:**
- 5/5: Suitable for official guideline document
- 4/5: Professional conference standard
- 3/5: Acceptable, needs refinement
- 2/5: Content good, presentation amateur
- 1/5: Guideline content weak
- 0/5: No regulatory framework

### 4. vs Garr Reynolds (Presentation Zen) — Score: X/5

**Criteria:**
- **Signal vs Noise:** Apenas o essencial
- **Regra dos 3:** Max 3 conceitos/bullets por slide
- **Restraint:** Resistir "mais é melhor"
- **Breathing room:** 5-6% padding mínimo

**Critical failures (auto -0.5 each):**
- ❌ >3 conceitos principais
- ❌ Chartjunk (gradientes, sombras, 3D)
- ❌ Cognitive overload (densidade excessiva)
- ❌ Elementos decorativos sem função

**Deductions:**
- 4-5 conceitos: -0.3
- Breathing room <5%: -0.3
- Gradientes sutis: -0.2
- Bullets desnecessários: -0.2

**Scoring guide:**
- 5/5: Zen master — cada pixel essencial
- 4/5: Excellent restraint, minor excesses
- 3/5: Good, poderia remover 20%
- 2/5: Signal competing with noise
- 1/5: Cluttered
- 0/5: Visual chaos

---

### 5. vs Edward Tufte (Visual Display) — Score: X/5

**Criteria:**
- **Data-ink ratio:** ≥80% elementos carregam informação
- **Zero chartjunk:** Sem gradientes, sombras, 3D decorativo
- **Small multiples:** Comparações facilitadas
- **Layering:** Hierarquia em camadas claras

**Critical failures (auto -0.5 each):**
- ❌ Data-ink ratio <70%
- ❌ Gradientes decorativos
- ❌ 3D charts
- ❌ Comparações difíceis (estruturas diferentes)

**Deductions:**
- Data-ink 70-79%: -0.2
- Sombras sutis: -0.2
- Grid lines proeminentes: -0.2
- Estrutura inconsistente: -0.4

**Scoring guide:**
- 5/5: Tufte would approve
- 4/5: Excellent data-ink, minor decorations
- 3/5: Good, some chartjunk removível
- 2/5: Information obscured by decoration
- 1/5: Decoration dominates
- 0/5: No clear data

---

### 6. vs Nancy Duarte (Resonate) — Score: X/5

**Criteria:**
- **Contrast creates meaning:** Visual difference = conceptual difference
- **Sparkline narrative:** Bridge para próximo slide
- **Emotional connection:** Pathos + logos
- **Visual metaphor:** Abstrato → concreto

**Critical failures (auto -0.5 each):**
- ❌ Sem contraste visual (tudo mesmo peso)
- ❌ Sem transição/bridge
- ❌ Dados frios sem contexto humano
- ❌ Teoria abstrata sem grounding

**Deductions:**
- Contrast sutil (<20% diferença): -0.3
- Bridge ausente/genérico: -0.2
- Exemplo sem conexão emocional: -0.3
- Dados sem interpretação humanizada: -0.3

**Scoring guide:**
- 5/5: Audience engaged AND informed
- 4/5: Strong storytelling, minor gaps
- 3/5: Informative but clinical/distant
- 2/5: Data present, no narrative
- 1/5: Cold presentation
- 0/5: Information dump

# PART 2: DESIGN ESSENTIALS

## Color System (Turner Palette)

**80/15/5 Rule:**
- **80%** Ivory background (#F9F8F4)
- **15%** Navy structure (#0B1320)
- **5%** Gold emphasis (#DDB944)

**Supporting colors:**
- Clinical Teal: #1F766E (GRADE elements)
- Blue: #2563EB (links, secondary emphasis)

**Critical rules:**
- Navy = authority (headers, critical info)
- Gold = emphasis (key numbers, highlights)
- Teal = clinical decision points
- White cards = primary content
- Navy cards = critical distinctions

---

## Typography

**Font pairing:**
- **Titles:** Georgia serif (elegance + authority)
- **Body:** Lato sans-serif (clarity + modern)

**Size hierarchy:**
```
h1: 8.5vw (capa)
h2: 4vw (section headers)
body: 1.4vw (main text)
label: 1vw (overlines)
caption: 0.85vw (citations)
```

**Critical rules:**
- Max 3 font sizes per slide (excluding capa)
- Line height: 1.4-1.5 for body text
- Letter spacing: 0.4vw for uppercase labels

---

## Layout Systems

### Grid 3-Column (default)
```
1.15fr | 0.95fr | 0.95fr
```
- Column 1: Primary content (35%)
- Columns 2-3: Supporting cards (32.5% each)

### Grid 2-Column (comparative)
```
1fr | 1fr
```
- Equal weight comparisons

### Single Column (title slides)
```
100% centered
```

**Breathing room:**
- Padding: 5-6% minimum
- Gap between cards: 2-2.5vw
- Border radius: 0.8vw (cards)

---

## Card Components

### Standard Card (White)
```css
background: #FFFFFF
border-radius: 0.8vw
padding: 2vw
box-shadow: 0 0.5vw 1.5vw rgba(0,0,0,0.03)
border: 1px solid rgba(0,0,0,0.05)
```

### Emphasis Card (Navy)
```css

```

### Citation Box
```css
background: #F8F9FA
padding: 1vw
border-left: 0.4vw solid var(--teal)
font-size: 0.85vw
```

## Badge System

**3-tier emphasis:**
```
HIGH:   background gold, color navy
MEDIUM: background teal, color white  
LOW:    background gray, color navy
```

**Usage:**
- HIGH: GRADE certainty, primary recommendations
- MEDIUM: Supporting evidence
- LOW: Caveats, limitations

# PART 3: WORKFLOW

## Step 1: Message Definition
- [ ] Define ONE primary message per slide
- [ ] Identify 2-3 supporting points max
- [ ] Frame in clinical decision terms

## Step 2: Content Hierarchy
- [ ] Primary (40% visual weight): Main message
- [ ] Secondary (30%): Supporting data
- [ ] Tertiary (30%): Context/caveats

## Step 3: Layout Selection
- [ ] 3-column: Complex comparisons
- [ ] 2-column: Binary choices
- [ ] 1-column: Title/transition slides

## Step 4: Component Assembly
- [ ] Cards for distinct concepts
- [ ] Badges for emphasis levels
- [ ] Citation boxes for sources

## Step 5: Visual Polish
- [ ] Check contrast ratios (3+ levels visible)
- [ ] Verify breathing room (5-6% padding)
- [ ] Validate data-ink ratio (≥80%)

## Step 6: Benchmark Scoring
- [ ] Self-score against all 6 benchmarks
- [ ] Target: ≥24/30 (80%)
- [ ] Iterate if <20/30

# PART 4: COGNITIVE PRINCIPLES (CONSOLIDATED)

## Adult Learning (Knowles)
**Core principles:**
1. **Autonomy:** Audience directs their learning → provide choices/paths
2. **Experience:** Leverage existing knowledge → bridge to familiar concepts
3. **Readiness:** Timely content → address immediate clinical needs
4. **Problem-centered:** Not theory → real cases, real decisions

## Cognitive Load (Sweller)
**3 types of load:**
1. **Intrinsic:** Complexity of content (unavoidable)
2. **Extraneous:** Poor design (minimize this)
3. **Germane:** Learning process (maximize this)

**Reduction strategies:**
- Intrinsic: Progressive disclosure, chunking
- Extraneous: Remove decorations, clear hierarchy
- Germane: Worked examples, dual coding (text + visual)

## Multimedia Learning (Mayer)
**Essential principles:**
1. **Coherence:** Less is more (remove extraneous)
2. **Signaling:** Highlight essential (color, size, position)
3. **Spatial contiguity:** Related elements near each other
4. **Redundancy:** Don't read slide verbatim

## Dual-Process Theory (Kahneman)
**System 1 vs System 2:**
- System 1 (fast): Patterns, visuals, simple math
- System 2 (slow): Complex reasoning, statistical inference

**Design implications:**
- Critical numbers: Large, prominent (System 1 capture)
- Complex logic: Step-by-step disclosure (System 2 support)
- Comparisons: Side-by-side structure (reduce cognitive effort)

# PART 5: EVIDENCE-BASED FRAMEWORK

## GRADE Certainty Levels

**4-tier system:**
1. **HIGH:** Further research unlikely to change estimate
2. **MODERATE:** Further research likely important impact
3. **LOW:** Further research very likely important impact  
4. **VERY LOW:** Estimate very uncertain

**Factors that LOWER certainty:**
- Risk of bias
- Inconsistency
- Indirectness (PICO mismatch)
- Imprecision
- Publication bias

**Factors that RAISE certainty:**
- Large effect size
- Dose-response gradient
- All plausible confounders reduce effect

## Visual Encoding for GRADE

**Badges:**
```
HIGH:      Gold badge, navy text
MODERATE:  Teal badge, white text
LOW:       Gray badge, navy text
VERY LOW:  Light gray badge, dark gray text
```

**Context boxes:**
- Sempre incluir razão para downgrade/upgrade
- Listar estudos principais (autor + ano)
- Especificar limitações concretas

## Indirectness Visualization

**PICO mismatch framework:**
```
Research question:  P₁ I₁ C₁ O₁
Available evidence: P₂ I₂ C₂ O₂
```

**Visual treatment:**
- Matches (✓): Green checkmark
- Mismatches (≠): Red indicator + explanation
- Proxies (≈): Yellow indicator + justification

# APPENDIX: ANTI-PATTERNS

**Content:**
- ❌ >3 main concepts per slide
- ❌ Generic examples ("considere estatina")
- ❌ Vague benefits ("reduz risco")
- ❌ Missing sources

**Visual:**
- ❌ Gradientes decorativos
- ❌ 3D charts
- ❌ <5% breathing room
- ❌ >3 font sizes (excluding capa)

**Typography:**
- ❌ ALL CAPS body text
- ❌ Line height <1.3
- ❌ Text over images (low contrast)

**Layout:**
- ❌ Asymmetric grids (sem razão conceitual)
- ❌ Elementos flutuantes (sem estrutura)
- ❌ Cards com padding inconsistente
